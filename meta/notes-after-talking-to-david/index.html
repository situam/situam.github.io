notes after talking to david

It starts from a fascination with soft fascinations - a richness in slowly-shifting subtlety and nuance, environments that offer restorative, not tiring, qualities (reference A.R.T.). and though promiment in visuals, also in sound - ambient sounds having noted influence on experience (reference proposals - emergence in slow-evolving soundscape, on minimalism, vertical time, masking, etc.). and wanting to experience this often and with a more or less active role.

(note to self) as an aside
how to work with these in a real-time way? (it's hard to engage in processes that aren't real-time)
how to build a system that works for me?
how to organize recordings in a way that they can be easily returned to? to understand/retrieve/hear-again the recordings with context they were made in?
how to develop a research working attitude

  • website as a working space - since it easily supports changing environments, sharing
  • just start developing, trying, documenting everything
  • make a roadmap, a place to keep/document also ideas that come up along the way and plans for making them real

The work is then about setting up containers and letting moments emerge. Meandering - in a physical sense, slowly changing environments, and stopping at the places in-between, mediating in real-time the experience with the immediate soundscape. Being, listening, playing, capturing, processing, and afterwards, of listening to recordings, is also real-time, then juxtaposed with a new time and place.

Session Entry

After a session, recordings are collected from field recorder and iphone into a folder, a script creates a session entry. This holds the recordings, offers annotation posibilities for time-based media (video/sound), and a place for text entry - insights, feedback, note to self, areas for improvement.

From here the relevant components can be moved to more suitable locations (under plans, or further developed into modules), but most importantly, the thoughts are recorded and collected in a place to be found again. to remember and remember to remember.
